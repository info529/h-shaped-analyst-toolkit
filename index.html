The PACE Framework

A structured approach to planning that acknowledges uncertainty and prepares for it systematically.

P

Primary

Your first-choice plan under normal conditions. This is the route, timing, and approach you intend to use when everything goes as expected.

A

Alternate

A near-equivalent option when your primary becomes unavailable. Similar effort, different path. Used when conditions shift moderately.

C

Contingency

A fallback for when both primary and alternate fail. Requires more compromise but keeps the trip viable and the group safe.

E

Emergency

The last resort. This plan prioritizes safety above all objectives. Evacuation routes, emergency contacts, and survival protocols live here.

Why PACE Matters Outdoors

The wilderness does not accommodate poor planning. Weather changes, trails close, injuries happen, and gear fails. PACE gives you a decision-making structure before stress compromises judgment.

  • Reduces decision fatigue when conditions deteriorate
  • Creates shared understanding among group members
  • Forces honest assessment of risks during the planning phase
  • Provides clear escalation paths without panic
  • Distinguishes inconvenience from genuine emergency
  • Builds confidence through preparation, not optimism
